Aston Martin could be getting ready for an initial public offering that could value the famous car manufacturer at as much as £5 billion, according to Reuters

Aston Martin have not yet decided on an IPO in London, a listing in New York or sale of stock to a private investor, according to the carmaker’s CEO.

Various reports earlier in the year indicated that the firm was set to hire Deutsche Bank, Goldman Sachs and JP Morgan to prepare a possible London stock market listing.

The automaker, which is best known as the preferred car of James Bond, made £87 million ($121 million) in 2017. Sales hit a record £876 million ($1.2 billion), a 48% improvement over the previous year.
